( Voise ) Transforming Text into Speech with Ease. The Journey Behind Our Voice-Based Web App.

Project Details

The Voice-Based Web App is a digital platform designed with accessibility and user experience at its core, aimed at transforming written text into spoken words through the integration of multiple Text-to-Speech (TTS) engines. The app leverages the capabilities of industry-leading TTS services such as Google TTS, ChatGPT TTS, Amazon Polly, and Microsoft Azure, providing users with a range of voice options, each with its own unique qualities.

The driving force behind this project was the need to create a versatile tool that could cater to a broad spectrum of users, including those with visual impairments, learning, By offering a seamless and intuitive interface, the app aims to make the process of converting text to speech.

The Problem Statement

Despite the availability of various Text-to-Speech tools, the landscape for TTS technology is fragmented and often fails to meet the diverse needs of users. Existing tools typically offer limited voice options and lack flexibility, forcing users to settle for a one-size-fits-all approach. This is particularly problematic for individuals with specific needs. These users require a tool that is not only reliable and high-quality but also easy to navigate and customize.

User Research

Findings

User Experience:

Users struggled with the lack of a unified, intuitive interface across different TTS tools.

Limited Customization:

Existing tools offered minimal customization, making it difficult for users to achieve the desired speech output.

Overwhelming Options:

Some users felt overwhelmed by the complex settings and multiple steps required to convert text to speech, leading to a steep learning curve.

Brainstorming

Unified Interface Design:

Design a cohesive, user-friendly interface that integrates all TTS engines seamlessly.

Accessibility-First Approach:

Implement key accessibility features such as high-contrast mode, screen reader compatibility.

Streamlined User Journey:

Simplify the user flow by minimizing the number of steps required to convert text to speech.

Color Theory

#509EE7 to #5C68E8 (Linear Gradient)

This gradient forms the primary visual theme of the app, providing a dynamic and engaging background. The cool blue tones evoke a sense of trust, reliability, and calmness, which are essential for a tool that users rely on for accessibility.

#FFFFFF (White)

White is used for the background and text areas to ensure high readability and contrast. It creates a clean and minimalistic look, making the content stand out and the interface easy on the eyes.

#252525 (Dark Gray)

Dark gray is used for text and icons on lighter backgrounds. It offers a strong contrast against white, ensuring legibility while maintaining a modern and professional appearance.

#1976D2 (Deep Blue)

This deep blue serves as an accent color, highlighting important buttons, links, and interactive elements. It draws attention without being overly aggressive, ensuring that users can easily find and engage with key features of the app.
